# GTM.md — Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact Target Users

**Primary:** Engineering leads and CTOs at startups (10–100 people) who manage multiple AI tool subscriptions but have no central view of what's being spent or why.

**Secondary:** Finance managers at mid-size tech companies who get surprise invoices from AI tools they didn't approve.

**Not our user (yet):** Solo developers, enterprise procurement teams, non-technical founders.

## How to Get First 100 Users With $0

**Week 1–2 — Warm network**

- Post a "I built this" thread on X with a real audit screenshot
- Submit to Hacker News Show HN on a Tuesday morning
- Share in 3 Slack communities you're already in
- DM 20 CTOs/engineering leads you know personally

**Week 3–4 — Earned distribution**

- Post on LinkedIn: "We audited 10 startup AI stacks. Here's what we found" with real anonymised data
- Submit to Product Hunt (prepare assets in advance)
- Write one blog post: "How much is your team actually spending on AI?" — SEO long tail

**Week 5–8 — Community compounding**

- Answer relevant questions on Reddit and HN with genuine help, link SpendLens when relevant
- Find 5 startup communities on Slack/Discord and offer free audits
- Ask every user for one referral after they complete their audit

**Goal:** 100 signups before spending a single dollar on ads.

---

## Most Effective Distribution Channel

**Hacker News Show HN** — engineering leaders read it, it's free, and a good post compounds for 48 hours. One well-timed Show HN with a real insight in the headline can drive 500–2,000 unique visitors.

Second best: **LinkedIn posts with real data** — finance and ops people share these.

---

## What Successful Early Traction Looks Like

- 100 audits completed in first 30 days
- 30%+ of users return within 7 days (team sharing the report)
- 15+ email leads captured from results page
- At least 3 unsolicited shares or screenshots on social media
- One inbound request from a company with 50+ people

---

## Trigger Moments

These are the moments when a user is most likely to search for or share a tool like SpendLens:

- Their OpenAI or Anthropic API bill suddenly spikes
- The finance team asks engineering to justify AI spending
- Multiple AI subscriptions start overlapping across teams
- A startup begins scaling from 5 → 20+ employees and loses visibility into tooling costs
- Engineering leaders compare Cursor, Copilot, Claude, and ChatGPT costs for consolidation
- Founders search for:
  - “How much should startups spend on AI tools?”
  - “Reduce AI API costs”
  - “Best alternative to Cursor enterprise”
  - “AI tool spend benchmark”
